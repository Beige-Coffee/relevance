Transjective Relations

Vervaeke introduces transjectivity as his primary metaphysical category for moving beyond the subject-object dichotomy that has dominated Western philosophy since Descartes. Relevance, affordances, meaning, religio, and the sacred are all transjective, neither merely projected by the mind onto a neutral world, nor passively detected in an independently existing world, but real co-constituting relations between organism and environment in which both poles are mutually realized. Grounded in 4E cognition and the biology of niche construction, transjectivity provides a naturalistic account of how meaning can be genuinely real without requiring supernatural ontology. Students will learn to distinguish transjectivity from both subjectivism and objectivism, understand its grounding in relevance realization and co-evolutionary fittedness, and see how it underwrites the sacred, symbols, and religio.

Prerequisites: The Four Kinds of Knowing

Module 1

Neither Projected Nor Detected

Define transjectivity as the mode of relation that is neither subjective projection nor objective detection, using Vervaeke's account of relevance as the central example.

Exposition

Episode 31 · Embodied-Embedded RR as Dynamical-Developmental GI
“I want to propose a term to you. I want to argue that relevance is in this sense, transjective (writes this on the board). It is a real relationship between the organism and its environment. We should not think of it as being projected. We should not think of it as being detected.”
Episode 38 · Agape and 4E Cognitive Science
“Is it therefore just pointing to something that's romantically dwelling within my subjectivity? No. It's pointing to caring about something that is inherently transjective”
Episode 38 · Agape and 4E Cognitive Science
“Relevance realization is not something in me. It's not something in the world. It emerges in the affordances that are co-created by the world in myself.”

Socratic seeds

When a hammer is relevant to you as you build a shelf, is that relevance something you project onto a neutral hammer, or something you detect in the hammer independently of you? What is wrong with both answers?
hammer affords hammering only for beings with a building projectrelevance requires both organism and environmentneither pure projection nor pure detection
Vervaeke contrasts transjective with 'romantic' (subjective). What does it mean to say that caring about something 'romantically dwells within subjectivity', and why does he reject this?
romantic account makes meaning merely personaltransjectivity means the relationship is real not merely feltRR tracks real features of organism-environment interaction
If relevance is transjective, what does that imply about whether meaning can be genuinely real in a scientific worldview?
meaning is real but not supernaturaltransjective relations are real relationsnaturalistic account that restores meaning without supernaturalism

Misconception branches

If: Transjective means intersubjective, something agreed upon by multiple people.
Then: Transjectivity refers to the real relational structure between organism and environment, independent of agreement among subjects; it is not a social consensus claim.
If: Acknowledging that relevance is transjective is a retreat into subjectivism.
Then: Transjectivity is a claim that the relationship itself has real structure grounded in the biology of niche construction and the cognitive science of relevance realization, it is neither objectivism nor subjectivism.

Check for understanding

beauty may be transjective rather than purely subjectivereal relationship between organism and worldnot projection but not objective detection eitherRR tracks real features

Misconception branches

If: Transjectivity requires some kind of mystical or non-physical ontology.
Then: Transjectivity is grounded in the biological and cognitive science of niche construction and enaction, it is a fully naturalistic account.

Transjectivity, Meaning, and the Sacred

“The sacred is the Transjective relationship between the combinatorially explosive nature of Reality - that reality is ultimately a no-thing-ness, that ultimately it is not a thing that you can frame. Reality will always transcend your framing.”

Misconception branches

If: If meaning is transjective, then everyone's meaning is equally valid since it all depends on the organism-environment relation.
Then: Transjectivity does not collapse into relativism, the relationship has real structure that can be more or less optimal, more or less self-deceptive; wisdom is the cultivation of the most optimal transjective relations.
