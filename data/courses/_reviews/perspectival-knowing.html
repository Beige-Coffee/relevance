Mini-course · 2 modules

Perspectival Knowing

After this course, students will be able to explain what perspectival knowing is, knowing constituted by what it is like to occupy a particular perspective, encoded in one's salience landscape, and why it is irreducible to propositional reports about one's perspective. Students will understand why Pascal's distinction between the 'spirit of geometry' and the 'spirit of finesse' diagnoses the modern loss of perspectival knowing, and why transformative experiences (mindfulness, gnosis, awakening) operate precisely at this level. This concept is pivotal in the series because it shows how two people can agree on all the propositions about a situation yet inhabit it completely differently, and why that difference matters more for wisdom, meaning, and therapy than any propositional disagreement. Perspectival knowing sits between procedural and participatory knowing in the foundational hierarchy.

Module 1

Salience Landscape as the Content of Perspectival Knowing

Explain what perspectival knowing is by examining how the salience landscape constitutes what it is like to occupy a perspective.

Exposition

Episode 1 · Introduction
“There's the kind of knowing of what it is like to be having this experience right now.”
First introduction, perspectival knowing as the 'what it is like' dimension of experience.
Episode 10 · Consciousness
“Part of what's happening, part of what consciousness is doing, is it's creating a salience landscape for me.”
The salience landscape is the mechanism through which perspectival knowing is implemented.

Misconception branches

If: Perspectival knowing is just subjective bias, it is less reliable than objective propositional knowing.
Then: Vervaeke argues perspectival knowing is a genuine kind of knowing constituted by what it is like to occupy a perspective. It is not mere bias but a structured epistemic achievement that propositional reports about one's perspective cannot replace.
If: Perspectival knowing is the same as emotional response, if you feel something strongly, that is perspectival knowing.
Then: Perspectival knowing is the structural salience landscape that makes emotional response intelligible in the first place. Emotion depends on perspectival knowing, not vice versa.

Module 2

The Loss of Perspectival Knowing in the Scientific Revolution

Explain Pascal's diagnosis of the modern collapse of the spirit of finesse into the spirit of geometry, and why this constitutes a loss of perspectival knowing.

Exposition

Episode 22 · Descartes vs. Hobbes
“And so Pascal is pointing out that what has been lost in the scientific revolution is all these other kinds of knowing and being, and these are the kinds of knowing and being that he found present in the transformative experience that he had.”
Pascal's personal transformative experience gave him perspectival and participatory knowing that the scientific method cannot capture.
Episode 17 · Gnosis and Existential Inertia
“you don't know what it is like cause that's the core of you don't know what your salience landscape will be like when you eat this fruit until you have eaten the fruit.”
Perspectival knowing cannot be anticipated, it must be undergone.

Socratic seeds

Pascal distinguished the spirit of geometry (methodical, propositional) from the spirit of finesse (intuitive grasp of complex situations). Vervaeke treats this as a distinction in kinds of knowing. What kind of knowing does the 'spirit of finesse' require that geometry alone cannot provide?

Misconception branches

If: Recovering perspectival knowing means rejecting science or returning to pre-scientific worldviews.
Then: Vervaeke is seeking an integration, 4E cognitive science offers a third-generation scientific framework that can accommodate perspectival and participatory knowing within a naturalistic account. The goal is not to reject science but to expand its self-understanding.
