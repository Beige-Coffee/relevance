Higher States of Consciousness

This course examines higher states of consciousness — the subset of altered states experienced as more real than ordinary reality — and explains why they pose a serious philosophical challenge rather than a curiosity for psychiatry. After completing this course, you will be able to describe the characteristic features of higher states (ontonormativity, ineffability, systematic insight), explain why 30–40% of the population reports having had such experiences across all cultures and historical periods, and articulate Vervaeke's account of their rational justification: not through propositional content but by optimizing relevance realization machinery. Higher states are pivotal in the series arc because they are what religious traditions have pointed to as the destination of the transformative practices Vervaeke studies — and understanding them naturalistically is essential for a non-supernaturalist response to the meaning crisis.

Prerequisites: Altered States of Consciousness

Module 1

What Makes a State 'Higher': Ontonormativity and the Sense of More-Realness

Articulate the defining feature of higher states of consciousness — that they are experienced as more real than ordinary reality — and what the term 'ontonormativity' captures.

Exposition

Episode 10 · Consciousness
“I call this the problem of the 'Ontonormativity.' Remember that 'ontology' has to do with the structure of reality. Normative is when things are placing a demand on you to be better, to improve.”
Vervaeke's coinage combining ontological disclosure with normative demand
Episode 29 · Getting to the Depths of Relevance Realization
“people claim to have the most profound sense of meaning and it is precisely ineffable. They reliably, across traditions, across historical contexts, claim that it is not conceptual, it can't be grasped categorically and they use the language of hereness and nowness to describe it”
The ineffability and cross-cultural character of higher states

Socratic seeds

Imagine someone returning from a deep meditative retreat who insists that the experience was 'more real than this room.' What would it even mean for one experience to be more real than another — and why might someone make that claim?
heightened sense of reality as phenomenological factnot just vividness but ontological seriousnesschallenges ordinary assumptions about what real means
Vervaeke coins the term 'ontonormativity' — combining ontology (what is real) with normative (what demands change). Why would an experience of heightened reality also carry a demand to transform your life?
contact with deeper reality demands responsenormative force flows from ontological disclosurenot just insight but imperative
30–40% of the population across all cultures reports having had a higher state experience. How do you interpret that cross-cultural frequency — does it suggest something real being accessed, something universal in human neurology, or something else?
cross-cultural universality requires explanationcould be neurological or ontological or bothnot explainable as cultural artifact alone
People who have higher states consistently describe them as ineffable — beyond language. But they also consistently describe them using similar language (presence, unity, light, peace). Is there a tension in those two facts?
ineffable yet cross-culturally similarlanguage approximates but does not captureshared deep structure beneath linguistic differences

Misconception branches

If: Higher states are just more intense or vivid versions of normal experience.
Then: Vervaeke's point is that higher states involve a qualitative restructuring of the salience landscape and the sense of self — they are not ordinary experience turned up, but a different kind of realness that demands a response.
If: Higher states are automatically veridical — what they reveal is reliably true.
Then: Vervaeke is careful to say their epistemic value depends on whether the insights they afford survive critical integration with waking cognition; the experience of heightened reality does not guarantee that the specific content is accurate.

Check for understanding

What does 'ontonormativity' mean — and why does the fact that higher states feel more real than ordinary experience create a philosophical puzzle rather than a simple answer?
ontonormativity = experiencing heightened reality that demands life transformationpuzzle is whether the disclosure is veridicalcannot dismiss as mere feeling because normative demand is too compelling
Module 2

Ineffability: Beyond Propositional Capture

Explain why higher states resist propositional description and what that tells us about the kinds of knowing they involve.

Exposition

Episode 29 · Getting to the Depths of Relevance Realization
“people claim to have the most profound sense of meaning and it is precisely ineffable. They reliably, across traditions, across historical contexts, claim that it is not conceptual, it can't be grasped categorically and they use the language of hereness and nowness to describe it”
Cross-cultural ineffability as a diagnostic feature of higher states

Socratic seeds

If something is truly ineffable — beyond language — how can you even report having had it? Is there a paradox in saying 'it was ineffable'?
meta-linguistic reporting possible even if content resists descriptionknowing-that it was significant even if knowing-what is beyond wordsperspectival knowing can be reported without propositional capture
People across cultures describe higher states using spatial metaphors — 'hereness,' 'nowness,' 'presence.' Why might spatial-temporal language be the closest approximation, even when the experience transcends ordinary space and time?
spatial language is the most basicpresence and immediacy are root categorieslanguage reaches limit but still approximates through roots
William James argued that mystical states have 'noetic quality' — they feel like genuine knowledge of something, not just a feeling. But they are also ineffable. How can something be both a form of knowing and beyond propositional capture?
participatory and perspectival knowing are real but non-propositionalnoetic quality = sense of contact with something realfour kinds of knowing not reducible to propositional

Misconception branches

If: If an experience is ineffable, it cannot be studied scientifically or evaluated rationally.
Then: Vervaeke argues that the cross-cultural regularities of higher states — their consistent features, their normative demands, their cognitive effects — are empirically investigable even when their content resists propositional capture.

Check for understanding

Why is ineffability a philosophically significant feature of higher states rather than just a failure of vocabulary — and what does it suggest about what kinds of knowing are involved?
not vocabulary failure but propositional knowing's limitparticipatory and perspectival knowing involvednoetic quality without propositionality
Module 3

Systematic Insight: Transframing the Framing Process

Distinguish systematic (transframing) insight — which transforms the competence for relevance realization — from ordinary insight that reframes a particular problem.

Exposition

Episode 40 · Wisdom and Rationality
“it not only is reframing the problem. It is transforming my competence so it is a transframing insight. It is a systematic insight.”
Vervaeke's definition of transframing insight that characterizes higher states

Socratic seeds

When you solve a puzzle by finding the right framing, that's ordinary insight. But when the experience makes you permanently better at solving puzzles — or more generally at seeing what matters — what kind of change is that?
competence-transforming vs. problem-solvingsystematic vs. local changetransframing vs. reframing
Higher states are often described as making everything make sense — not just one thing, but reality as a whole. How does that phenomenology fit with the idea of a 'systematic' or 'transframing' insight?
salience landscape as a whole is restructuredframing capacity upgraded not just one frame changedsense of total intelligibility
If a higher state produces a systematic insight — transforming your competence for relevance realization — how would you test whether that transformation was real and lasting versus merely temporary feeling?
behavioral evidence of changed saliencelasting changes in what is noticed and cared aboutintegration into waking cognition needed

Misconception branches

If: Higher states are just more intense versions of the insight you get when solving a difficult puzzle.
Then: Ordinary insight reframes a particular problem within an existing competence; the systematic insight of higher states transforms the competence itself — it is a transframing, not just a reframing.

Check for understanding

What is the difference between a reframing insight and a transframing insight — and why does Vervaeke argue that higher states of consciousness afford the latter rather than just the former?
reframing solves one problemtransframing transforms the framing competencehigher states restructure the whole salience landscape

Higher States as Engine of Gnosis

Articulate why higher states of consciousness function as the engine that empowers the participatory transformation required to overcome existential entrapment.

Exposition

Episode 38 · Agape and 4E Cognitive Science
“we can respond to existential entrapment by the cultivation of gnosis, which can be empowered by a core capacity for realizing higher states of consciousness”
Higher states as the engine of gnosis and the pathway out of existential entrapment

Socratic seeds

Existential entrapment is being stuck and stupefied — unable to imagine or inhabit an alternative worldview. Why might a higher state of consciousness be more useful for escaping that trap than propositional argument?
argument cannot override existential paralysishigher states can break out of entrenched framingparticipatory transformation needed not just new beliefs
Vervaeke says gnosis can be 'empowered' by higher states. What is the relationship — is the higher state the same as gnosis, or the condition for it, or the fuel that drives it?
higher state provides cognitive flexibilitygnosis is the process using that flexibilityhigher state enables but does not guarantee gnosis
If higher states are the engine of the most profound human transformations, how do you explain why some people who have dramatic altered-state experiences do not show lasting transformation?
state without context and integration yields no lasting changegnosis requires the right ritual and community scaffoldthe experience is necessary but not sufficient

Misconception branches

If: Higher states are restricted to drug-induced experiences.
Then: Vervaeke emphasizes that flow, deep meditation, and ritual practice can all occasion higher states of consciousness — altered states from psychedelics are one pathway, not the defining one.

Check for understanding

Why does Vervaeke argue that the cultivation of higher states of consciousness is relevant to addressing the meaning crisis — what role do they play in gnosis and in overcoming existential entrapment?
higher states provide the cognitive flexibility that gnosis requiresbreak through existential entrapment that argument cannotnaturalistic basis for the transformations religions sought
