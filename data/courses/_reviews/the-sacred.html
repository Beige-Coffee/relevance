Mini-course · 3 modules

The Sacred

Vervaeke proposes a naturalistic account of the sacred, grounded in relevance realization and 4E cognition, that does not require supernatural metaphysical commitment yet does justice to the function and phenomenology of sacredness across traditions. The sacred is understood as the transjective relationship between the inexhaustible no-thing-ness of reality (always exceeding any frame) and the inexhaustible no-thing-ness of relevance realization in oneself. Sacredness functions as a meta-meaning system that homes individuals against horror and absurdity, and the symbol, mythos, and ritual are the primary vehicles by which it is activated. Students will learn to distinguish the sacred from the supernatural, understand its meta-meaning function via Geertz, grasp its transjective character via Vervaeke's own account, and see how the numinous marks the transgressive edge of sacred experience. This concept is pivotal in the series arc: it provides the experiential target for the 'religion that is not a religion.'

Prerequisites: Transjective Relations, The Meaning Crisis

The Sacred as Meta-Meaning System

Explain how sacredness functions as the meta-meaning system that homes individuals against horror and absurdity, drawing on Geertz's account.

Exposition

“one of the functions of sacredness is the Meta-Meaning process of homing us against horror (Meta-Meaning => Sacredness => Homing Against Horror) where horror would be to be overwhelmed by loneliness.”
Episode 1 · Introduction
“Most horror movies aren't horror. We'll talk about what horror really is and what people experience when they're actually experiencing horror. Their sense of grip on reality is being undermined and people find that, as you can imagine, terrifying.”

Socratic seeds

Vervaeke says sacredness 'homes us against horror.' What is the difference between horror in this technical sense and ordinary fear? And why would a meta-meaning system be the antidote?
horror is loss of grip on reality itselfordinary fear has a specific object and responsemeta-meaning provides the frame within which all other meaning operates
Geertz argues that sacredness is what makes individual meaning systems possible, it is meta-meaning. What would happen to all your particular meaningful projects if the meta-meaning system failed?
absurdityagent-arena decouplingloss of all significancethe meaning crisis at its deepest level
If sacredness homes us against horror, does engaging with what is sacred carry any risk of horror itself?
numinous edges on horrortransgression of the ordinary framesacred and horror are closer than they appear

Misconception branches

If: Sacredness is just a strong feeling of awe or reverence.
Then: Sacredness has a structural function, meta-meaning, homing against horror, and a transgressive edge (the numinous) that mere feelings of awe do not capture.
If: The sacred is identical to the religious, you need a religion to access the sacred.
Then: Vervaeke argues that the sacred as meta-meaning system operates prior to and independently of any particular religious tradition; the goal of 'religion that is not a religion' is to re-engage the sacred without supernatural metaphysical commitment.

Check for understanding

If someone's sense of the sacred collapsed entirely, if nothing felt genuinely meta-meaningful, what would their experience be like, and why couldn't propositional argument restore it?
horror and absurdity at the most fundamental levelmeta-meaning is not propositionalrequires participatory and perspectival transformationreligio is the machinery not a doctrine
Module 2

The Sacred as Transjective Inexhaustibility

Articulate Vervaeke's account of the sacred as the transjective relationship between the inexhaustible no-thing-ness of reality and the inexhaustible no-thing-ness of relevance realization.

Exposition

Episode 35 · The Symbol, Sacredness, and the Sacred
“The sacred is the Transjective relationship between the combinatorially explosive nature of Reality - that reality is ultimately a no-thing-ness, that ultimately it is not a thing that you can frame. Reality will always transcend your framing.”
Episode 33 · The Spirituality of RR: Wonder/Awe/Mystery/Sacredness
“I'm not claiming that Relevance Realization is God, that's ridiculous. I'm not doing that. But what I'm saying is [that] wonder and awe - which are often directed towards things like God - are ways that Costa is arguing in which we disclose the Relevance Realization and it's spiritual significance to us”
Episode 46 · Conclusion and the Prophets of the Meaning Crisis
“the enhancement of religio is already, and remember the role of wonder, potentially even awe here? It's taking us into sacredness (writes Sacredness below Religio), the notion of sacredness that I already have argued and articulated for.”

Socratic seeds

Vervaeke says reality is 'combinatorially explosive' and therefore ultimately a 'no-thing-ness.' What does he mean, and why does this make the sacred inexhaustible rather than finite?
reality always exceeds any framingno finite description captures the wholeinexhaustibility is the source of sacredness not a particular sacred object
The sacred is described as a transjective relationship. What is the difference between saying 'the sacred is in me (subjective)', 'the sacred is in the world (objective)', and 'the sacred is transjective'?
not projectednot merely detectedreal relationship between organism and environmentrelevance realization as the meeting point
If someone feels a sense of the sacred while standing in a cathedral and another person feels it while standing in a forest, is the same thing being experienced? How would Vervaeke's account accommodate this?
particular mythos indispensable for individualsunderlying structure of inexhaustible transjectivity is the samespecific symbol or place need not be metaphysically necessary

Misconception branches

If: Vervaeke's naturalistic account reduces the sacred to merely a feeling produced by the brain.
Then: The transjective account argues the sacred is a real relationship between organism and environment, not subjective projection and not objective detection; it is a structural feature of relevance realization meeting the inexhaustible nature of reality.
If: The sacred is identical to religio.
Then: Religio is the participatory machinery; the sacred is the phenomenological encounter with the inexhaustible that religio makes possible, they are closely related but distinct.

Check for understanding

Why does Vervaeke locate the source of sacredness in inexhaustibility rather than in any particular sacred object, text, or place, and what are the implications for the 'religion that is not a religion'?
particular symbols are indispensable but not metaphysically necessarysacred can be accessed without supernatural commitmentinexhaustibility means ongoing encounter rather than fixed doctrine

Socratic seeds

If the sacred is not supernatural, can it still perform the same psychological and social functions that supernatural religion historically performed?
meta-meaning function is structural not metaphysicalreligio can be activated without supernatural commitmentreligion that is not a religion as the target
A traditional believer might say: 'Without God, how can anything be truly sacred?' How might Vervaeke respond?
sacredness grounded in inexhaustibility of reality and RRparticular mythos indispensable to individuals without being metaphysically necessarythe question presupposes only supernatural can be source of sacredness
