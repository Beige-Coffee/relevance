Altered States of Consciousness

This course examines altered states of consciousness, the radical transformations of the salience, presence, and depth landscapes that shamanic and contemplative traditions deliberately cultivated and that modern research has begun to study scientifically. After completing this course, you will be able to explain how altered states differ from ordinary insight (they are insights of consciousness rather than in it), how disruptive strategies produce cross-neural integration that enables cognitive exaptation, and how the Upper Paleolithic cognitive revolution may have been driven by shamanic psychotechnologies. Altered states are a foundational concept in the series arc: they are the vehicle by which higher states of consciousness are reached, and understanding their cognitive mechanics is essential for evaluating whether the religious transformations Vervaeke traces throughout history have any naturalistic warrant.

If: Altered states are just a degraded or impaired version of normal consciousness.

Exposition

Socratic seeds

Module 4

Cognitive Exaptation and the Upper Paleolithic Transition

Explain how shamanism functioned as a set of psychotechnologies for cognitive exaptation, and why Vervaeke connects this to the Upper Paleolithic transition.

Exposition

Episode 2 · Flow, Metaphor, and the Axial Revolution
“the Upper Paleolithic transition was probably driven by the way shamanism was a set of pscyho-technologies for altering states of consciousness to cognitively exapt the enhanced abilities”
The core historical claim about shamanism and the cognitive revolution

Socratic seeds

Exaptation in biology means a feature that evolved for one purpose gets used for another, the way feathers evolved for insulation and were later exapted for flight. What would 'cognitive exaptation' look like, can you think of a human mental capacity that gets used for purposes it was not originally for?
repurposing evolved capacitieslanguage exapting social cognition circuitsmathematical thinking using spatial reasoning
Around 40,000 years ago there was a sudden explosion of symbolic art, ritual objects, and complex tools in human culture, the Upper Paleolithic transition. Why might altered states of consciousness, rather than genetic changes, explain this sudden leap?
no genetic change required if cultural technology explained itshamanic psychotechnologies enabled new cognitive usescultural transmission of new cognitive practices
Altered states allowed early humans to use sensorimotor pattern-recognition machinery in new symbolic and artistic domains. Does this suggest that meaning-making is, at root, a kind of redeployment of capacities that evolved for something else?
meaning-making as cognitive exaptationsymbolic thought built on evolved perceptionculture extends biological machinery

Misconception branches

If: The Upper Paleolithic cognitive revolution required genetic mutation, a biological change in the human brain.
Then: Vervaeke's argument is that cultural psychotechnologies, specifically shamanic practices for altering consciousness, can explain the cognitive revolution without requiring new genetics; the tools changed how existing capacities were used.
