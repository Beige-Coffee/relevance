Mini-course · 4 modules

Relevance Realization

This course develops Vervaeke's central technical construct: relevance realization, the non-propositional, embodied process by which the mind-body system dynamically selects what matters out of a combinatorially explosive sea of available information. Students will be able to explain why relevance cannot be explained by representations or a central executive, articulate the key constraints any adequate theory must satisfy (non-circularity, auto-poetic grounding, scale-invariant self-organization), and connect relevance realization to meaning, agency, and the four kinds of knowing. In the series arc, relevance realization serves as Vervaeke's naturalistic foundation for consciousness, wisdom, religio, and the possibility of responding to the meaning crisis, making it one of the most load-bearing concepts in the entire lecture series.

Why Relevance Cannot Be Computed: The Combinatorial Explosion

Socratic seeds

If you cannot explain relevance by invoking a rule or a homunculus that 'knows' what matters, where could relevance come from? What kinds of processes might produce it without presupposing it?
self-organizing processesauto-poetic groundingbiological goals not represented targetsnon-intentional dynamics

Misconception branches

If: Relevance realization is a conscious process of filtering information, we decide what is relevant.
Then: Relevance realization is sub-representational and pre-categorical; it is the process that makes conscious cognition possible, not a product of it.
If: A sufficiently powerful computer could, in principle, solve the relevance problem by checking everything.
Then: The combinatorial explosion makes exhaustive search not merely impractical but in principle impossible for any real-time agent, the relevant subset must emerge through self-organization, not search.

Aspectuality and Salience Tagging: How Relevance Is Structured

Explain what aspectuality is and how enactive demonstrative reference (salience tagging) underlies all categorical cognition.

Exposition

Episode 29 · Getting to the Depths of Relevance Realization
“a set of features that are relevant to each other and then a set of features that have been structurally functionally organised so that they have co-relevance, is then relevant to me. That's what an aspect is”
Definition of aspectuality: organized co-relevance
Episode 29 · Getting to the Depths of Relevance Realization
“I need demonstrative reference I need enactive demonstrative reference before I can do any categorisation. Look, if I'm going to categorize things I need to mentally group them together.”
Pre-categorical salience tagging is the condition for all categorization

Socratic seeds

When you look at a face and immediately see it as sad, are you first seeing a collection of features (downturned mouth, furrowed brow) and then inferring sadness, or is the sadness somehow immediate? What does your answer suggest about how relevance is structured?
gestalt prior to featuresaspectualityco-relevanceperception not inference
Vervaeke says that before you can categorize something you must first 'point at' it, mark it as a here-now focus. Can you have a concept without first being able to enactively single out instances? What would concepts be without that?
demonstrative reference prior to conceptindexical before categoricalenaction grounding abstractionpre-linguistic salience
An aspect is described as a set of features organized so they have co-relevance. How does this explain why changing one feature of a situation (e.g., finding out a smile was sarcastic) can reorganize how all the other features seem?
gestalt reorganizationco-relevancereframinginsight as aspect shift
If relevance is always perspectival, always relative to an agent, does that mean it is purely subjective? Or is there a sense in which the relevance is real?
transjective not purely subjectivereal affordancesorganism-environment co-creationnot arbitrary

Misconception branches

If: Relevance is a property of objects in the world, independent of any agent.
Then: Relevance is transjective, it emerges in the co-created relationship between organism and environment, neither purely 'in' the world nor purely projected by the mind.
If: Aspectuality means we see representations of aspects, not aspects themselves.
Then: Representations presuppose aspects, they foreground a subset of features as relevant but do not generate relevance from scratch. Aspectuality is a constraint on representations, not something produced by them.

Auto-poetic Grounding and Scale-Invariant Self-Organization

Explain why relevance realization must be grounded in constitutive goals internal to a self-organizing system, and why it must operate simultaneously at multiple levels.

Exposition

Episode 29 · Getting to the Depths of Relevance Realization
“The goals that are the originating source of Relevance Realisation have to be internal to the Relevance Realisation process.”
Auto-poetic grounding: goals cannot be externally represented targets
Episode 29 · Getting to the Depths of Relevance Realization
“Relevance Realisation has to be happening at multiple levels of cognition in a simultaneous, self-organizing fashion. That's why it can lead to insight.”
Multi-level self-organization as both constraint and explanation for insight
Episode 38 · Agape and 4E Cognitive Science
“relevance realization is inherently interested in itself because, as I've argued, that's constitutive of it being a self-organizing, self-correcting, self-optimizing, self-developing, evolving process”
RR's reflexive self-interest as central to its nature

Socratic seeds

If a thermostat 'wants' to maintain 70 degrees only because a human set it, does it have genuine goals? What would it take for a system to have goals that are genuinely internal to it, not assigned from outside?
constitutive vs assigned goalsauto-poiesisself-organizationlife vs mechanism
Relevance realization happens at multiple levels simultaneously, from individual features up to holistic gestalt. Why might this multi-level, simultaneous operation be necessary for insight? Could insight happen if only one level were active at a time?
insight requires cross-level reorganizationsimultaneous processinggestalt shiftwhy RR enables breakthrough
Vervaeke says relevance realization is 'inherently interested in itself.' What does it mean for a cognitive process to be self-interested, and why is this reflexivity constitutive of wisdom rather than narcissism?
reflexive self-monitoringself-correctionwisdom as calibrationhigher-order relevance
If the goals driving relevance realization must be internal rather than externally imposed, what does this imply about whether a purely designed AI system could ever genuinely realize relevance in the same way a living organism does?
life vs AIconstitutive vs programmed goalsauto-poiesis requirementbiological grounding

Misconception branches

If: Relevance realization is a single mechanism located in one brain region, like a relevance-filter.
Then: Relevance realization operates self-organizingly at multiple levels simultaneously, it is a distributed, scale-invariant process with no single locus, which is precisely why it can produce insight through cross-level reorganization.
If: The goals that drive relevance realization can be specified by an external designer or programmer.
Then: For relevance realization to be non-circular, the originating goals must be constitutive, internal to the self-organizing living system, not represented targets assigned from outside.

Meaning, Relevance, and Agency: The Constitutive Triangle

Misconception branches

If: Meaning is a feeling or belief, something purely subjective that exists independently of relevance or action.
Then: Vervaeke argues meaning, relevance, and agency are constitutively entangled: meaning is the sense of connectedness constitutive of relevance, and agency is the relational enactment of that connection, none of the three can be adequately described in isolation.
