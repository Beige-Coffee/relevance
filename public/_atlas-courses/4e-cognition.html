Mini-course · 5 modules

4E Cognition

This course introduces the third-generation cognitive science framework that underlies Vervaeke's entire response to the meaning crisis. You will be able to articulate why mind is not a computer running symbol-manipulation software, and instead how cognition is embodied (arising through sensorimotor coping), embedded (co-constituted with environment through niche construction), enacted (something organisms do rather than passively receive), and extended (distributed through psychotechnologies and social networks). Understanding 4E cognition is essential because it provides the naturalistic worldview in which concepts like relevance realization, participatory knowing, and religio can be coherently situated, replacing the Cartesian ghost-in-machine picture that has made the meaning crisis harder to diagnose and harder to solve.

Modules

  2. Embeddedness: Organism and Environment Co-Constitute Each Other
  3. Enaction: Cognition Is Something We Do

Module 1

Embodiment: Mind Arises Through Coping, Not Computation

Articulate why the mind is not a program running on a biological computer, and how immediate sensorimotor coping grounds all cognition.

Exposition

Episode 38 · Agape and 4E Cognitive Science
“Mind is not programmed software or rule-bound manipulation of symbols. Instead, the mind arises through immediate coping with the world.”
Vervaeke's core statement of the embodiment thesis

Socratic seeds

Think about riding a bicycle or catching a ball. Can you describe what you are doing in those moments purely in terms of rules your brain is following? What might be missing from a purely rule-based account?
rule-following fails to capture copingbody and world integratedimplicit know-how
If a robot had a perfect internal model of the world but no body, would it understand what it means to be hungry or to balance? What does that thought experiment suggest about the relationship between mind and body?
body is constitutive not incidentalsensation and action entangledrepresentation insufficient
Vervaeke argues that mind 'arises through immediate coping.' What do you think 'immediate' means in this context, and why would directness matter to what cognition is?
no mediating symbolic layer requireddirect sensorimotor engagementcoping before theorizing
How does the idea that mind is embodied change how you would think about improving your thinking, would you change your practices, not just your beliefs?
practice transforms cognitionbody-level habituation mattersmindfulness and flow relevant

Misconception branches

If: Embodiment means the brain is important to cognition, which everyone already knew.
Then: Vervaeke's claim is stronger: cognition is constitutively dependent on sensorimotor interaction with the world, the body is not just the hardware but partly constitutes what thinking is.
If: 4E cognition denies that the brain uses any internal representations.
Then: The claim is not that representations are unimportant, but that they are not the origin or sufficient basis for cognition, representations arise from and serve the coping loop, not the other way around.

Check for understanding

In your own words, what is wrong with thinking of the mind as a program and the brain as its computer, and what picture does embodied cognition offer instead?
rule-manipulation cannot ground immediate copingmind arises through sensorimotor engagementconstitutive role of body
Module 2

Embeddedness: Organism and Environment Co-Constitute Each Other

Explain the difference between mere environmental influence and the co-constituting organism-environment loop Vervaeke calls niche construction.

Exposition

Episode 31 · Embodied-Embedded RR as Dynamical-Developmental GI
“The world and the organism are co-creating co-determining co-evolving the fittedness.”
Summary formulation tying embeddedness to relevance realization

Socratic seeds

A fish shapes the water it lives in and is shaped by it, its fins form in response to currents, and its movement creates eddies that other fish respond to. In what sense is the fish 'in' its environment versus 'one with' its environment?
organism-environment boundary is porousco-constitution not mere interactionniche construction
When a city is built, the humans build the city and the city shapes the humans who grow up in it. Is this just environmental influence on behavior, or something more? What makes it 'more'?
niche construction is bidirectionalenvironment selects and shapestransjective loop
Vervaeke distinguishes being 'in' an environment from being 'embedded' in it. What work does that distinction do, and why might it matter for understanding meaning?
embedded means co-constitutingmeaning is relational not internalcoupling not containment

Misconception branches

If: Embeddedness just means the environment affects behavior, like how a cold room makes you think more slowly.
Then: Embeddedness in Vervaeke's sense is a co-constituting loop: organism and environment shape and select each other through niche construction, not merely influence each other causally.
If: Niche construction is only a biological concept relevant to evolution, not to everyday cognition.
Then: Vervaeke applies niche construction to cognition itself, every use of a tool, every psychotechnology, and every cultural institution is a form of cognitive niche construction that co-shapes the mind using it.

Check for understanding

How does niche construction illustrate the difference between being environmentally influenced and being embedded, and why does this difference matter for how we understand cognition?
bidirectional shaping and selectionco-constituting loop not one-way causationorganism-environment boundary is fluid

Enaction: Cognition Is Something We Do

Articulate the claim that cognition is enacted through the sensorimotor loop rather than passively received from the environment.

Exposition

Episode 38 · Agape and 4E Cognitive Science
“cognition is an inherently something we enact”
Concise statement of the enaction thesis

Socratic seeds

When you are reading these words, are you receiving meaning from the page, or doing something active to produce that meaning? What would the enactivist say about the difference?
meaning constituted in actionnot passive receptionsensorimotor loop active
Think about color perception. Is 'red' a property of a tomato, a property of your brain, or something enacted in the interaction between you and the tomato? What evidence would help you decide?
color as interaction not intrinsic propertyperception enacted not receivedorganism-environment coupling
The word 'enacted' contains the word 'act.' What does that grammatical link suggest about the relationship between agency and knowing in this framework?
knowing tied to doingcognition is performativeagent-arena coupling

Misconception branches

If: Enaction means that all knowledge is constructed by the individual mind and therefore there is no objective reality.
Then: Enaction does not deny a real world, it argues that cognition is constituted in the transaction between organism and world, not that the world is invented by the mind.

Check for understanding

Why does Vervaeke say cognition is 'something we enact' rather than something that happens to us, and what consequences does that have for thinking about learning and transformation?
cognition is active not receptivesensorimotor loop constitutivetransformation requires doing not just knowing
Module 4

Extension: Mind Beyond the Skull

Explain how psychotechnologies, artifacts, and distributed cognition extend mind beyond the individual brain.

Exposition

Episode 38 · Agape and 4E Cognitive Science
“our cognition is not in our head, but it's extended through our interactions, through the world, through our psychotechnologies, through distributed cognition”
Vervaeke's statement of cognitive extension

Socratic seeds

When you use a notebook to offload your memory, or use a calculator to solve arithmetic, is that tool part of your cognition or separate from it? How would you decide?
tool extends cognitive processboundary of mind is functional not anatomicalextended mind thesis
Language itself is a psychotechnology, it shapes what you can think and remember. In what sense might language be part of your mind rather than just a medium your mind uses?
psychotechnology co-constitutes cognitionlanguage restructures saliencenot merely a medium
If your cognition is extended through your social networks and cultural tools, what does that say about what is lost when those networks are disrupted, say, during social isolation or cultural collapse?
extended cognition socially dependentloss of psychotechnologies damages cognitionmeaning crisis connection

Misconception branches

If: Cognitive extension is just a metaphor, in reality the brain is where all real thinking happens.
Then: Vervaeke, following Andy Clark, argues this is a substantive claim: if the functional role of a process in cognition is what makes it cognitive, then external tools performing that function are genuinely part of cognition.
If: Extension makes the four E's independent competing theories rather than aspects of one framework.
Then: The four E's mutually presuppose the organism-environment loop, each highlights a different aspect of the same fundamental relational structure of cognition.

Check for understanding

In what sense is cognitive extension a naturalistic explanation of how wisdom traditions and psychotechnologies work, and how does this differ from a merely sociological account?
psychotechnologies extend and restructure cognitionnaturalistic not just culturaldistributed cognition real not metaphorical
Module 5

Deep Continuity: Mind Within the Natural Order

Articulate why 4E cognition places mind within the natural order rather than above it, and why this matters for addressing the meaning crisis.

Exposition

Episode 38 · Agape and 4E Cognitive Science
“the mind and the world are not disconnected. They are in a deep continuity of embeddedness and enactive processing.”
The deep continuity hypothesis as summary of the 4E framework

Socratic seeds

Descartes argued that mind and matter are fundamentally different substances. What problems does that create for understanding how we know anything about the world, and how does 4E cognition respond?
Cartesian gap between mind and world4E dissolves the gap via participationknowing as constitutive contact
If mind is continuous with life and life with self-organizing physical processes, does that reduce mind to mere mechanism, or does it invite a richer understanding of mechanism itself?
continuity is not reductionself-organization has genuine normativitymeaning emerges from natural order
Vervaeke argues that 4E cognition provides the worldview within which religio, the reconnection that addresses the meaning crisis, can be situated naturalistically. What would a non-naturalistic account of religio look like, and why might it be unsatisfying?
non-naturalistic account requires supernatural4E naturalizes participationscientifically situated without being reductionist

Misconception branches

If: 4E cognition is anti-scientific because it challenges the computational model that mainstream cognitive science uses.
Then: 4E cognition is a third-generation cognitive science, it expands the scientific account of mind beyond computational internalism using empirically motivated frameworks from dynamical systems, phenomenology, and enactivism.

Check for understanding

Why does Vervaeke need a 4E cognitive science framework to address the meaning crisis, what would be inadequate about a traditional computational account of mind as a backdrop for his project?
computational internalism cannot account for participatory knowing4E provides naturalistic basis for religiotransjective participation requires organism-environment continuity
