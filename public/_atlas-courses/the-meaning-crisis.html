Mini-course · 4 modules

The Meaning Crisis

Vervaeke diagnoses a pervasive cultural-historical phenomenon: modern people experience a profound sense of disconnection, from themselves, each other, the world, and a viable future. The series opens by establishing that this crisis is not merely political or economic but deeply cognitive and historical, rooted in the erosion of meaning-making resources built up since the Axial Age. Students will learn to distinguish the three interlocking crises (meaning, environmental, socioeconomic), identify the symptomatology (rising suicide, nihilism, bullshit), and understand the two-legged structure of the problem: historical factors that have dismantled Western meaning-making traditions, and perennial vulnerabilities endemic to the same relevance-realization machinery that makes humans adaptively intelligent. This framing concept is the entry point for the entire series.

Misconception branches

If: The meaning crisis is caused primarily by social media or political dysfunction.
Then: Vervaeke argues these are symptoms or co-drivers of a deeper cognitive-historical-cultural crisis rooted in the erosion of axial-age meaning-making, not its causes.
If: The meaning crisis is a crisis of religious belief, people just need to believe in God again.
Then: The crisis is a crisis of religio (participatory binding to the world) and of the practices that cultivated it; reducing it to a loss of doctrine misses the cognitive depth of the problem.

Misconception branches

If: The meaning crisis is a recent phenomenon caused by specific modern technologies like smartphones.
Then: Vervaeke traces the roots to the Protestant Reformation and scientific revolution, centuries before smartphones, as they dismantled the integrated axial-age worldview.
If: The meaning crisis is unique to modernity; ancient people didn't face it.
Then: The perennial problems are endemic to the very machinery of relevance realization and have always been a threat; what modernity did was remove the historical safeguards that wisdom traditions built.

Misconception branches

If: Providing people with more factual information about the meaning crisis will help them address it.
Then: Because the crisis is a crisis of religio and of the four kinds of knowing, not just propositional knowing, information alone cannot address it; what is needed is transformation of character, perspective, and participation.
