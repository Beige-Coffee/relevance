Mini-course · 3 modules

Salience Landscape

This course develops Vervaeke's concept of the salience landscape, the dynamically structured field of standing-out and standing-back that consciousness produces through featurization, foregrounding, configuration, and framing. Students will be able to explain how the salience landscape operates as a pre-reflective topography that shapes what enters deliberate cognition, articulate why it is neither a spotlight nor a passive mirror, and connect optimal grip to the agent-arena relationship. In the series arc, the salience landscape is the phenomenological counterpart to relevance realization: where relevance realization names the sub-representational process, the salience landscape names the structured experiential terrain it generates, the field within which wisdom, self-deception, and transformation become possible.

Prerequisites: Relevance Realization

The salience landscape is described as 'dynamic', it shifts as the agent-arena relationship evolves. Can you think of a time when a situation suddenly looked completely different because one element changed? What had shifted in your salience landscape?
gestalt shiftreframinglandscape change with new informationaspect seeing vs feature seeing

Misconception branches

If: The salience landscape is a fixed spotlight of attention that the agent controls voluntarily.
Then: The salience landscape is a dynamic topography that shifts as the agent-arena relationship evolves; it operates pre-reflectively and shapes what enters reflective cognition, making it prior to voluntary attention rather than a product of it.
If: Salience is purely subjective preference, whatever someone personally cares about becomes salient.
Then: Salience tracks real affordances in the environment co-created by organism and world; it is transjective, neither purely in the world nor purely projected by the mind, but co-arising in their dynamic relationship.

Module 3

Optimal Grip: Calibrating the Salience Landscape

Explain what optimal grip is and how the dynamic adjustment of the salience landscape to achieve optimal grip connects to the agent-arena relationship and to the practice of wisdom.

Exposition

Episode 10 · Consciousness
“I'm taking my salience landscape and I'm using it to get an optimal grip on things. Not maximal. And 'grip' is meant here as a metaphor.”
Optimal grip: neither too distant nor too close, neither too abstract nor too detailed

Socratic seeds

When you're reading, you don't focus on every individual letter, nor do you stand back so far that you can't see the words. You find an optimal level of grain. Why 'optimal' and not 'maximal', why is maximum detail not always best?
optimal vs maximalgrain of perceptiontask-relative salienceoverdetail as disorienting
In a conversation, sometimes you need to track the exact words someone says, and sometimes you need to track the overall emotional tone. How do you shift between these levels of grip, and what happens when you get the level wrong?
level-shifting in attentionwrong grain causes misunderstandingdynamic grip adjustmentagent-arena fit
Wisdom has often been described as knowing what matters in a situation and how much it matters. How does the concept of optimal grip connect to this characterization of wisdom?
wisdom as optimal salienceappropriate grainsituational calibrationwisdom not rules
Self-deception often involves having a salience landscape that is systematically distorted, some things loom too large, others are invisible. What would it take to recalibrate a distorted salience landscape? Can propositions do it?
self-deception as salience distortionrecalibration through practicepropositions insufficienttransformative practices

Misconception branches

If: The salience landscape is not reducible to explicit beliefs about what matters; it operates pre-reflectively.
Then: This is correct, but the implication is often missed: recalibrating a distorted salience landscape (as in self-deception or addiction) requires practices that operate at the sub-propositional level of the landscape itself, not merely new explicit beliefs.
