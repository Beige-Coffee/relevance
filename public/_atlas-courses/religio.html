Mini-course · 3 modules

Religio

This course develops Vervaeke's concept of religio — the deep, pre-institutional, transjective binding of agent and arena that underlies all individual acts of meaning-making and all religious and spiritual traditions. Students will be able to distinguish religio from both institutional religion and from its propositional counterpart credo, explain how religio functions as higher-order relevance realization, and articulate why the modern meaning crisis is partly a crisis of religio — the collapse of the lived participatory binding into mere creedal belief. In the series arc, religio is the concept that bridges cognitive science (relevance realization, agent-arena relationship) to the normative question of what wisdom practices must cultivate in order to address the perennial problems of human existence.

Prerequisites: Relevance Realization

Misconception branches

If: Religio is simply a feeling of the numinous or mystical awe.
Then: Religio is a cognitive-existential structure — the higher-order relevance realization that grounds all individual meaning systems — not a feeling, though such feelings may be its phenomenal expression.
If: Religio is religion — it is about belonging to or practicing within a religious tradition.
Then: Religio is pre-institutional and pre-propositional; it is the participatory binding that religious institutions have historically tried to activate, but it exists independently of any particular tradition.

Religio versus Credo: The Collapse That Created the Meaning Crisis

Articulate the distinction between religio and credo and explain how the historical collapse of religio into credo contributed to the modern meaning crisis.

“one of the things that wisdom is doing that's also really important is it's enhancing religio (writes Religio below Wisdom). That's a way in which you can powerfully—and we saw already the connection.”

Socratic seeds

Credo means creed — the propositional content of what one believes. Religio is the lived participatory binding. Can you imagine someone who sincerely assents to every doctrine of a religion but feels entirely empty, dead inside, disconnected from everything? What has that person lost?

Vervaeke argues that modernity has largely collapsed religio into credo — treating religion as primarily a matter of holding the right beliefs. What cultural, historical, or institutional forces might have produced this collapse?
Reformation and propositional faithscientific worldview reducing experienceloss of ritual practicesindividualism and belief
If recovering religio does not require adopting a particular credo, what would it require? What practices, conditions, or transformations might reactivate the binding that religio names?
practices over propositionsecology of psychotechnologiesparticipatory knowingtransformation not belief-change

Misconception branches

If: The meaning crisis can be solved by returning to creedal religion — by people believing the right things again.
Then: What is needed is recovering religio, not credo; the meaning crisis is a crisis of participatory binding, not of incorrect propositions, and it can be addressed through a 'religion that is not a religion' without metaphysical commitment.
If: Vervaeke's distinction between religio and credo is an attack on religious belief.
Then: Vervaeke explicitly respects the indispensability of particular creeds and myths for particular people; his critique targets the reduction of religion to credo divorced from the participatory practices that give creeds their existential power.

Misconception branches

If: Religio is a kind of spiritual self-absorption or navel-gazing — it is about the self.
Then: Religio is precisely the dissolution of ordinary self-absorption: it is the pre-egoic and post-egoic binding in which the sharp boundary between self and world is suspended, not reinforced.
