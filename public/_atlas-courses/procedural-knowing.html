Procedural Knowing

After this course, students will be able to explain what procedural knowing is, the knowing instantiated in skills, powered by implicit learning, irreducible to propositional description, and why it occupies a crucial middle position in the four-kinds-of-knowing hierarchy. Students will understand why no amount of propositional instruction can teach one to ride a bicycle, and what that reveals about the structure of competence and expertise. They will also understand why procedural knowing depends on perspectival knowing: executing a skill well requires knowing what it is like to be in this situation. This concept is directly relevant to the series' argument that wisdom is not a set of beliefs but a cultivated capacity, and to why transformative practices (meditation, philosophical dialogue, virtue ethics) cannot be replaced by information.

Misconception branches

If: Procedural knowing is mere muscle memory or automatic reflex, a lower, animal kind of knowing.
Then: Vervaeke is clear that procedural knowing is not mere reflex. It involves a complex coordination of implicit learning, situational awareness, and skill application, it is richer than propositional knowledge in important ways and the foundation on which much propositional knowing depends.
If: Knowing that you should do something is the same as knowing how to do it.
Then: This is precisely Aristotle's point about akrasia, you can know propositionally what you should do and still fail because you lack the procedural knowing (the cultivated character and skill) to enact it. Knowing-how and knowing-that are genuinely different kinds.

Module 2

Procedural Knowing Depends on Perspectival Knowing

Explain why effective skill application requires situational awareness, perspectival knowing, and how this dependency reveals the hierarchy of the four kinds of knowing.

Exposition

Episode 43 · Wisdom and Virtue
“your propositional knowing has to be grounded in your procedural abilities, which have to be grounded in your perspectival, which has to be grounded ultimately in your participatory.”
Explicit statement of the full dependency hierarchy, procedural depends on perspectival which depends on participatory.
Episode 36 · Religio/Perennial Problems/Reverse Eng. Enlightenment
“It's at the level of procedural and ultimately perspectival and participatory knowing.”
Shorthand for where transformative practices operate, not at the propositional tip of the hierarchy.

Socratic seeds

Vervaeke argues that wisdom is not a set of true beliefs but a cultivated capacity. How does the dependency hierarchy explain why wisdom cannot be transmitted through information?
wisdom operates at procedural-perspectival-participatory levelsinformation is propositional and highest derivativecultivation requires practice transforming lower levels

Misconception branches

If: Propositional knowing is the highest and most complete form of knowing, procedural knowing is just applied knowledge.
Then: Vervaeke's hierarchy inverts this assumption: propositional knowing is the most derivative kind, depending asymmetrically on procedural, perspectival, and participatory knowing. 'Applied knowledge' misses the fact that skill requires its own non-propositional foundations.
