The Perennial Problems

Vervaeke argues that alongside the historical drivers of the meaning crisis there is a second leg: the perennial problems. These are cross-cultural, transhistorical vulnerabilities endemic to the very relevance-realization machinery that makes humans adaptively intelligent. The same system that enables insight, learning, and meaning-making can generate parasitic processing, modal confusion, the reflectiveness gap, absurdity, anxiety, and alienation. Every culture has developed ecologies of psycho-technologies, wisdom traditions, religions, philosophical practices, to address these problems. The meaning crisis emerges precisely when historical factors have invalidated those traditions, leaving the perennial problems uncountered. Students will learn to identify each perennial problem, understand how it arises from adaptive machinery, and see why each requires its own targeted counteractive practice.

Prerequisites: The Meaning Crisis

Misconception branches

If: The perennial problems are moral failings, they happen when people choose badly.
Then: They arise precisely because the machinery that makes us intelligently adaptive can be turned against itself; they are structural vulnerabilities, not moral failures.
If: The perennial problems are unique to modernity.
Then: They are inherent in the relevance-realization machinery present across all cultures and times; what modernity has done is remove the traditional safeguards against them.

Modal confusion involves pursuing being-mode needs (love, maturity, meaning) through having-mode strategies (buying things, acquiring status). Can you think of examples where this leads people astray?

Parasitic processing is described as a self-reinforcing loop that 'takes on a life of its own and takes life from us.' What makes it hard to break out of by willpower alone?

Misconception branches

If: Modal confusion is simply a mistaken belief that can be corrected by being told the right way to think about needs.
Then: Modal confusion is an existential misalignment between mode of engagement and the need being pursued; correcting it requires transforming the participatory knowing that grounds the agent's world, not just updating beliefs.

The Reflectiveness Gap and Absurdity

Define the reflectiveness gap (second-order cognition failing to track first-order processes) and absurdity (agent-arena decoupling), and identify the practices that address each.

Exposition

Socratic seeds

The reflectiveness gap is when our second-order cognition (thinking about our thinking) fails to track what is actually happening in our first-order processing. How might this produce self-deception?
blindness to how we are actually motivatedpost-hoc rationalizationgap between espoused and enacted values
Vervaeke describes absurdity as the tennis player in the football arena, the agent's actions no longer make sense within the worldview. How is this different from mere confusion or uncertainty?
agent-arena decoupling not just cognitive errorexistential mode itself is brokennot a specific problem but a breakdown of meaning-making
Why does Vervaeke say flow addresses the reflectiveness gap? What does being in flow do to the relationship between first-order and second-order cognition?
flow reduces self-monitoring that creates the gaptight feedback closes the reflectiveness gapimplicit and explicit processing align in flow

Misconception branches

If: Absurdity is a philosophical position (like Camus's) that can be adopted or rejected.
Then: In Vervaeke's account, absurdity is a phenomenological diagnosis, the lived experience of agent-arena decoupling, not a theory; resolving it requires transframing, not philosophical argument.

Check for understanding

How are the reflectiveness gap and absurdity related, could one produce or worsen the other? What does the need for different practices (flow vs Prajna) to address them tell us?

Socratic seeds

Vervaeke distinguishes anxiety (pervasive, untethered threat-detection) from fear (of a specific threat). Why might addressing them require different approaches?
fear has an object and can be addressed directlyanxiety lacks an object and requires reframing the self's relation to threatinternalizing the sage shifts the perspective
Alienation is described as the breakdown of communitas, feeling fundamentally separated from others and from shared meaning. How does this connect to the meaning crisis more broadly?
religio is participatory and relationalcommunitas is the social form of religioalienation is the social dimension of the meaning crisis
Why does cultivating inner dialogue with an internalized sage address existential anxiety, rather than merely suppressing it or distancing oneself from it?
internalizing the sage provides a metacognitive regulatorsage perspective de-centers from anxious self-monitoringsophrosyne develops from this practice
Each perennial problem has its own targeted practice. What would be the danger of applying a single cure-all (say, just meditation) to all five perennial problems?
different mechanisms require different countermeasuresecology of practices not single techniquecounteractive dynamical systems must match the problem

Misconception branches

If: Alienation is primarily a sociological or economic problem caused by capitalism or inequality.
Then: While social conditions contribute, alienation in Vervaeke's sense is a breakdown in communitas, the participatory binding that grounds shared meaning, which is a perennial vulnerability of the relevance-realization machinery.
If: Addressing the perennial problems requires choosing one tradition (e.g., Buddhism or Stoicism) and following it exclusively.
Then: Vervaeke argues for an ecology of practices that targets distinct mechanisms; different problems require different countermeasures, and reverse-engineering enlightenment means mapping problems to specific psycho-technologies.

Check for understanding

Suppose someone addresses their modal confusion successfully through mindfulness but still experiences profound alienation. Why would the modal confusion solution not automatically resolve the alienation, and what would Vervaeke's framework recommend?
perennial problems are distinct with different mechanismsalienation requires communitas not satiecology of practices addresses each dimensionproblems interact but are not identical
